# DesktopTerrarium Visual Style Bible

## Target feeling
- A believable glass terrarium sitting on a warm desk in soft natural light.
- The overall mood should feel premium, calm, tactile, and alive.
- The terrarium should read as a real object first and a game surface second.

## Visual direction
- Style: stylized realism.
- Camera: fixed three-quarter hero shot with very subtle drift.
- Composition: asymmetrical, lush, layered, and dense enough that the glass vessel feels inhabited.
- Silhouette priority:
  - glass vessel outline
  - substrate layers
  - one hero plant
  - two supporting plants
  - hardscape accents
  - soft atmospheric haze and highlights

## Material goals
- Glass:
  - readable rim highlights
  - soft transparency
  - restrained specular response
  - visible wetness shifts during rain/fog
- Soil:
  - rich brown with damp variation
  - darker and slightly shinier under wet weather
- Moss:
  - soft green clusters with depth variation
  - slightly brighter and richer under moisture
- Stone and bark:
  - muted earth palette
  - rough enough to contrast the vessel
- Plants:
  - no flat fills
  - no bright synthetic greens
  - growth stages should change silhouette, density, and spread

## Lighting goals
- Day:
  - warm natural key light
  - soft neutral fill
  - subtle rim/specular read on the glass
- Evening:
  - warmer amber key with deeper desk shadows
  - stronger mood separation between vessel and background
- Night:
  - cooler ambient fill
  - low, readable specular on glass
  - no harsh black crush
- Weather:
  - rain increases wetness and glass readability
  - fog increases interior haze, but should never turn the scene milky
  - wind should animate foliage and debris, not overpower the vessel

## Composition rules
- Avoid isolated objects floating in empty space.
- Keep visible overlap between plants, moss, props, and substrate.
- Every plant should feel rooted in the substrate, not dropped onto it.
- The desk and backdrop should support the terrarium, not compete with it.
- Foreground accents are welcome, but should not block the main vessel silhouette.

## Plant staging rules
- Hero plant sits near center and carries the strongest vertical silhouette.
- Left support plant should arc outward and frame the composition.
- Right support plant should counterbalance with denser, lower mass.
- Stage progression must change shape:
  - stage 0: sparse, juvenile
  - stage 1: rooted and readable
  - stage 2: fuller branching/spread
  - stage 3: mature silhouette
  - stage 4: dense, premium hero look

## Critter rules
- Critters should feel grounded to the environment.
- Butterflies belong in canopy and glass-adjacent air paths.
- Beetles belong on soil, bark, and moss-adjacent routes.
- Movement should enhance the sense of life, not steal focus from the terrarium.

## What is placeholder today
- Current 3D visuals are procedural stand-ins built from Bevy primitives.
- They are useful for composition, lighting, anchor placement, and behavior integration.
- They are not the final art direction endpoint.

## Art replacement priorities
1. Glass vessel model and material.
2. Substrate kit: pebbles, charcoal, soil surface breakup.
3. Plant kits for fern, moss, and succulent stages.
4. Hardscape props: bark, stone, branch.
5. Critter models/cards.
6. Condensation, micro-particles, and refined weather FX.
